Work

United States Census Bureau

I just finished up my time as a software engineering intern at the U.S. Census Bureau as a 2019 Civic Digital Fellow for the Summer of 2019. I worked as a full-stack engineer to implement a dashboard for monitoring system usage statistics of AWS resources for the Disclosure Avoidance System (DAS). Click here to view the public repository. The DAS will be utilized by the 2020 decennial census to protect the privacy of all respondents. This is accomplished using differential privacy, which is a rigorous mathematical definition of privacy. The DAS is the largest scale application to have been developed utilizing a differentially private algorithm.

This opportunity was made possible by Coding it Forward which is a non-profit created to bring tech innovation to the federal government. My work was unvieled at the Civic Digital Fellowships's 2019 Demo Day, and the slides related to my work can be found here.

The skills I learned or refined through working on this project include AWS EC2 and S3, Linux, Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS, MySQL, CGI, and Git.

Rockwell Automation

I was employed at Rockwell Automation as a software engineering co-op from May 2018 to May 2019. I worked on Logix Designer, which is an application developed by Rockwell Automation to configure and program the entire Allen-Bradley Logix 5000 suite of programmable logic controllers.

My primary role was to maintain the Logix Designer code base, which is a large scale application written primarily in C++ dating back to the 1980's. I worked on an Agile team to write features, build unit/integration tests, solve anomalies, and write documentation within multiple domains of the code base. My favorite accomplishment was delivering a feature to the main File menu of the application.

Additional projects I worked on included a DevOps role in which I worked with Jenkins and PowerShell to automate processes, and a role with Rockwell Automation's Center for Excellence to build upon IBM's Collaborative Lifecycle Management tools.

The skills I learned or refined through my time at Rockwell Automation include C++, Java, Jenkins, PowerShell, Python, Git, and ClearCase.

Bio

Noah Houpt

I'm 21 years old, and am currently attending school at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland, OH.

I initially became fascinated with computers through my love of video games. I started with a Playstation 2 when I was 8 years old, from there I transitioned over to computer gaming. When I was 12, my family's desktop couldn't handle World of Warcraft which meant I wanted a PC with better specs. After some research, my family and I decided that the most economical option involved assembling a PC ourselves. After purchasing the individual PC components, I built my first ever PC. This process resulted in me dipping my toes into the world of computer science and engineering, and sparked the passion I have today of becoming a software engineer
